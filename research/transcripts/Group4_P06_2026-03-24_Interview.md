Interview transcript – P03 – Mohsin Ali

[Interviewer]: What is your current role?
[Participant]: I’m working as a digital marketing specialist.

[Interviewer]: Do you work remotely, in-office, or hybrid?
[Participant]: Fully remote.

[Interviewer]: How long have you been working remotely?
[Participant]: About two years now.

[Interviewer]: What did your first weeks of remote work feel like?
[Participant]: It felt flexible but also a bit isolating at first.

[Interviewer]: What were the first things you had to adjust to?
[Participant]: Managing my time, communication tools, and staying motivated.

[Interviewer]: What’s the biggest challenge of working remotely?
[Participant]: Staying focused and avoiding distractions at home.

[Interviewer]: What tools do you use daily?
[Participant]: Slack, Zoom, project management tools, and email.

[Interviewer]: When you need help, how do you usually ask for it?
[Participant]: I message teammates or set up a quick call.

[Interviewer]: Do you find communication easy or difficult?
[Participant]: It can be both—easy for quick things, harder for complex discussions.

[Interviewer]: What’s frustrating about remote communication?
[Participant]: Misunderstandings and delayed responses.

[Interviewer]: How do you stay organized?
[Participant]: I use task lists, calendars, and project tools.

[Interviewer]: What’s not ideal about your current setup?
[Participant]: Too many tools sometimes—it gets overwhelming.

[Interviewer]: How do you separate work and personal life?
[Participant]: I try to stick to working hours and take breaks.

[Interviewer]: What helps you stay productive?
[Participant]: Having a routine and a dedicated workspace.

[Interviewer]: Do you ever feel isolated?
[Participant]: Yes, especially when there’s less team interaction.

[Interviewer]: What would improve your remote work experience?
[Participant]: Better team engagement and fewer unnecessary meetings.

[Interviewer]: Have you used AI tools in your work?
[Participant]: Yes, mainly for content ideas and automation.

[Interviewer]: What do you like or dislike about them?
[Participant]: They save time, but sometimes need double-checking.

[Interviewer]: What are your top two challenges with remote work?
[Participant]: Communication gaps and maintaining focus.

[Interviewer]: What impact do these challenges have?
[Participant]: They slow down work and can cause stress.

[Interviewer]: One change that would make remote work better?
[Participant]: A more streamlined system for communication and tasks.
