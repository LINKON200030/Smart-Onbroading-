[Interviewer]: What is your current job role?
[Participant]: I’m working as an electronic engineer.
[Interviewer]: What industry is your company in?
[Participant]: Electronics and technology.
[Interviewer]: Roughly how big is the company?
[Participant]: Over 500 employees.
[Interviewer]: What did your first days or weeks look like?
[Participant]: I was excited but also a bit overwhelmed, especially understanding systems and
internal processes.
[Interviewer]: Top three things you needed to know early on?
[Participant]: System architecture, project requirements, and tools and software used.
[Interviewer]: What was the hardest part of getting started?
[Participant]: Understanding complex systems and how everything connects.
[Interviewer]: Did onboarding feel helpful?
[Participant]: Mostly helpful, but some parts felt like just ticking boxes.
[Interviewer]: When you need an answer, where do you go first?
[Participant]: Usually my manager or a senior engineer.
[Interviewer]: How often do you check more than one place?
[Participant]: Sometimes, depending on the issue.
[Interviewer]: What’s annoying about that?
[Participant]: It can waste time and cause confusion.
[Interviewer]: A time you couldn’t find something important?
[Participant]: Trying to locate updated technical documentation for a project.
[Interviewer]: How do you keep track of documents?
[Participant]: Using shared drives, notes, and some project tools.
[Interviewer]: What’s not great about your method?
[Participant]: No standard structure, so it gets messy.
[Interviewer]: What would you want on a home screen?
[Participant]: Tasks, key project updates, and contacts.
[Interviewer]: What info should be quick to access?
[Participant]: System specs, safety details, and project requirements.
[Interviewer]: How do you know info is correct?
[Participant]: I check official documents or confirm with team members.
[Interviewer]: What would’ve helped on your first day?
[Participant]: Clear instructions and a structured onboarding plan.
[Interviewer]: What makes onboarding feel personal?
[Participant]: When it’s tailored to your role and experience.
[Interviewer]: Did you know who to ask for help?
[Participant]: Yes, my manager and team.
[Interviewer]: Have you used AI tools at work?
[Participant]: Yes, quite often.
[Interviewer]: What’s good or bad about that?
[Participant]: It’s fast and helpful, but sometimes not fully reliable.
[Interviewer]: What reduces trust in AI?
[Participant]: incorrect information.
[Interviewer]: What should AI not handle?
[Participant]: Sensitive or critical decisions and the ones that need human input.
[Interviewer]: Worried about privacy with AI?
[Participant]: Yes, data security is important.
[Interviewer]: Top two problems?
[Participant]: Finding the right information and relying on others for simple issues.
[Interviewer]: What did these problems cost you?
[Participant]: Time and efficiency.
[Interviewer]: One change to improve starting a job?
[Participant]: A centralized system with all key information in one place.
