H1: We believe that a centralized, role-specific home screen will help employees across all departments because we heard in interviews that workers waste significant time juggling up to 20 different systems daily, navigating messy shared drives or constantly rotating data locations, and struggling to find a single source of truth for daily updates, tasks, and key department contacts.

H2: We believe that structured, step-by-step onboarding guides will help new hires because we heard in interviews that current onboarding often feels like generic "box-ticking", leaving employees confused about their core responsibilities and forcing them to "figure stuff out themselves", or leaving them waiting up to 17 days for an initial manager meeting just to understand their daily tasks.

H3: We believe that an AI tool that explicitly cites sources, removes "fake empathy," and guarantees data deletion will help employees handling sensitive tasks because we heard in interviews that users actively distrust AI when it hallucinates basic facts like days of the week, gives overly wordy or emotional answers, or lacks clear proof (such as a mandatory consent form) that confidential student, financial, or personal data is protected and will not be tracked.

H4: We believe that a rapid-search knowledge base with verified, up-to-date information will help frontline and support staff because we heard in interviews that employees urgently need quick, reliable access to critical details—such as a student's allergies and emergency next-of-kin contacts, updated technical documentation, or accurate drink specifications—but current systems are often conflicting or inaccurate, leading to stressful delays and mistakes during busy shifts.
